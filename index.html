Statistics for Suicide for Men in the United States

by Allen Jake Polintan

Mental Visualization Project focusing on the user to learn about current suicide statistics for men in the United States,
mental factors correlations that may contribute to mental issues, and to give users resources
to find Mental Resources such as their nearest crisis center / various mental health organizations.

This website will feature interactable line charts of suicide rates in the US, interactable histograms of age ranges for suicide count to see
who is most affected, scatterplots and small multiple line charts to show potential factors for these suicide statistics,
and a map of locations with crisis city centers across the United States.

The Suicide Rate and Count for Men has been Increasing

Select to change y-axis for the chart.

count : The count of suicides
crude_deathrate : The raw number of suicides per 100,000 people
agestd_rate : Adjust suicide rate for all age differences between population per 100,000 people

Takeaway: The suicide rate and count for men in the United States has been increasing

source: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/ronaldonyango/global-suicide-rates-1990-to-2022
sourced from: World Health Organization and World Bank Open Data

The Age Ranges That Are Most Affected By Suicide

Use the slider to view how suicide count is changing for different age ranges, year by year

Takeaway: The most affected age range in 2021 were adults between 25-34 years
All age ranges before 34 years of age increased in Suicide Count for Men

source: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/ronaldonyango/global-suicide-rates-1990-to-2022
sourced from: World Health Organization and World Bank Open Data

Drug Use Disorders Has Been on the Rise

Each disorder represents the percentage of people with that disorder in the US within that year

Takeaway: One key disorder that is increasing is Drug Use in the United States. Drug Use may be correlated with this rise of suicides

source: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/thedevastator/uncover-global-trends-in-mental-health-disorder
sourced from: Our World In Data

Employment Rate Affects Suicide Rates

Select to change y-axis for the chart. US has a black stroked line around its circle

employment : Employment Population Ratio
inflation : Inflation rate
gdp : The gdp of that country
population : The current population

Takeaway: The US has a low employment population rate compared to other countries which also may be correlated with this rise.
Inflation, Population, and GDP do not share a relationship with Suicide Death Rate per 100k.

source: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/ronaldonyango/global-suicide-rates-1990-to-2022
sourced from: World Health Organization and World Bank Open Data
